The Past Perfect
in Ireland

A journey through the Emerald Isle – and through grammar

Year 7 · English

Grammar at a Glance

Subject · had · past participle (3rd form)
Form Example
Affirmative ✓ She had visited Dublin before.
Negative ✗ They had not seen the cliffs yet.
Question ? Had he heard of the Book of Kells?

💡 Use the Past Perfect for actions that happened before another point in the past.

1
📖 Find the Past Perfect Reading – spot the grammar in context

Read the text about Ireland. The highlighted verbs are in the Past Perfect. Can you see why each one was used?

When Lena arrived in Dublin for the first time, she was amazed. She had only seen pictures of the city before, but nothing could have prepared her for the real thing.

Her class had read about the River Liffey in their English textbooks, but now she was standing on the Ha'penny Bridge, looking down at the water below.

By the time the group reached Trinity College, the guide had already explained the history of the Book of Kells twice. Lena was glad she had listened carefully on the bus.

In the evening, they drove along the Wild Atlantic Way. Lena had never experienced such dramatic cliffs before. The Cliffs of Moher took her breath away.

Think about it:
Why is "had only seen" used and not just "saw"? Because Lena seeing pictures happened before she arrived — it was earlier in the past. ✔
