Going-to Future
AI in School · Class 6
🚀 Introduction
AI is coming – and it has plans!

Have you ever heard a robot say: "I am going to help you with your homework." 🤖

The going-to future is used when:
  • you have already decided to do something
  • you can see from the situation that something is about to happen
🤖
"The AI is going to write your essay." → A plan has already been made.
📱
"Look! The screen is going to turn off." → You can see it happening right now.
🔧 Structure
How to build it

Three parts – always in this order:

Subject + am / is / are + going to + Infinitive
✔ Positive ✗ Negative ? Question
I am going to learn I am not going to learn Am I going to learn?
She is going to use She isn't going to use Is she going to use?
They are going to build They aren't going to build Are they going to build?
💡 Tip: The verb to be changes (am / is / are) – just like in the Simple Present!
📚 Examples
AI at school – real sentences

Read these sentences carefully. Can you spot the pattern?

🗓️
Our teacher is going to show us a new AI tool next week. → Plan: she has already decided this.
🎨
We are going to use AI to design our class poster. → The class has already made that plan.
😮
Watch out! The robot is going to drop the tablet! → You can see it in the situation right now.
🤔
Is the school going to buy new AI computers? → A question about a future plan.
🙅
I am not going to let AI write my homework! → A negative decision already made.
🧩 Notice: going to never changes – only am / is / are does!
