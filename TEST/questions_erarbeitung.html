In English there are two main types of questions:
  • Yes/No questions – answered with yes or no
  • Wh-questions – give more information (who, what, where …)
🏅 We will use sport and music contexts to practise both types.
✅ Yes/No Questions
How to build a Yes/No question

In a statement the verb comes after the subject. In a Yes/No question, we swap them:

Statement
Subject + do/does + not + Infinitive + …

Yes/No Question
Do / Does + Subject + Infinitive + … ?
💡 Important: Use Does for he / she / it – the verb has no -s!
Statement Yes/No Question
I play football. Do I play football?
You like music. Do you like music?
She plays the piano. Does she play the piano?
They train every day. Do they train every day?
🎤 Short answers: "Yes, I do." / "No, I don't." / "Yes, she does." / "No, she doesn't."
❓ Wh-Questions
Wh-Questions – get more details!

When yes or no is not enough, we use a question word (Wh-word) at the start:

Wh-word + do / does + Subject + Infinitive + … ?
Wh-WordAsks about…Example
Whatthing / actionWhat sport do you play?
WhopersonWho does she train with?
WhereplaceWhere do they practise?
WhentimeWhen does the concert start?
How oftenfrequencyHow often do you train?
WhyreasonWhy does she like hip-hop?
⚠️ Common mistake: Do NOT say: "What sport you play?" – the helper verb do/does must be there!

🏆 Summary
You've got it! 🎉
Yes/No Do / Does + Subject + Infinitive … ?

Wh-Q Wh-word + do / does + Subject + Infinitive … ?
Key rules to remember:
  • Do for I / you / we / they
  • Does for he / she / it – verb WITHOUT -s
  • Wh-word comes first, then do/does
  • Never forget the helper verb do/does!
